    • Watch Out For Nettles!

    8 DECEMBER 2022

    I was walking along a path at a farm when I saw this cute calf. I had to get a picture of it, so I stepped closer to the fence.

    Little did I know that there was a nettle branch right next to it! I brushed it as I stepped back onto the path.

    Big mistake. It hurt a lot, and kind of ruined the day for me. I still had fun, though, and I think the picture was worth it.

    Current Fiber Projects

    9 DECEMBER 2022

    I have a lot of yarn projects ongoing. My main one right now is a baby blanket for a friend. The baby was born in September, so I should probably finish it sooner rather than later.

    I'm also working on a fingering-weight scarf. It's a really pretty color, about the shade of gingko leaves. It's taking forever, but slow and steady wins the race, right?

    Some other projects I've been working on are a skein of handspun yarn, a brioche stitch scarf, and some others I'm probably forgetting.

    What Do I Use For My Art?

    9 DECEMBER 2022

    My art right now is mainly digital. I use a couple of different programs for it. My main one is Krita, which is open source and built around painting. I've also messed around in Photoshop, but I don't like it as much for digital art.

    My graphics tablet is an XP-Pen Deco Pro SW. It's a good size and easy to take around.

    When I do traditional art, it's mainly small doodles in the margins of my class notes with a mechanical pencil. Sometimes I do sketch something in a cheap sketchbook or on printer paper, but it's usually nothing fancy.
